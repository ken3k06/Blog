Let $k$ be a field and let $k^n$ denote the set of all $n$ - tuples:
$$ 
k^n = \lbrace a=(a_1,...,a_n) : a_i \in k \ \text{for all} \ i \rbrace
$$

We use the abbreviation 
$$
X = (x_1,...,x_n)
$$
so that we can conveniently denote the polynomial ring $k[x_1,...,x_n]$ by $k[X]$. A polynomial $f(X) \in k[X]$ determine polynomial function $k^n \rightarrow k$. 
Now let assume that we are working on infinite fields. 
**Definition 1.** If $f(X) \in k[X]$ and $f(a) = 0$ where $a \in k^n$ then $a$ is called a zero of $f(X)$. If $f(x)$ is an univariate polynomial then $x$ is called a root of $f$. 
**Proposition 1.** If $k$ is an algebraically closed field and $f(X) \in k[X]$ is not a constant then $f(X)$ has a zero.
(The proof is left as an exercise for Math student <("))
**Definition 2.** If $F$ is a subset of $k[X]$ then the affine variety defined by $F$ is 
$$
V(F) = \lbrace a \in k^n : f(a) = 0, \forall f(X) \in F \rbrace; 
$$
which mean $V(F)$ consists of all those $a \in k^n$ which are zeros of every $f(X) \in F$. 
**Proposition 2.** Let $k$ be a field and let $F,G$ be subsets of $k[X]$. 
i/ If $F \subseteq G \subseteq k[X]$ then $V(G)\subseteq V(F)$ 
ii/ If $F \subseteq k[X]$ and $I = (F)$ is the ideal generated by $F$, then 
$$ 
V(F) = V(I)
$$
Let $I$ and $J$ be two ideals of $k[X]$. An ideal generated by $I \cup J$ is called the sum of $I$ and $J$, we denote this by $I + J$. An ideal generated by the product of $fg$ for each $f\in I$ and $g\in J$ is called the product ideal of $I$ and $J$ and denote by $IJ$. 
Thus, 
$$
 \begin{array}{l} I + J = \lbrace f+g \ | f \in I, g \in J \rbrace  \\ IJ = \lbrace f_1g_1+...f_rg_r \ | f_i \in I,g_i\in J , r \geq1 \rbrace\end{array}
$$
Example: Given $I = (x,y^2), J = (y)$ are two ideals of $k[x,y]$. Calculate $I+J, IJ , I \cap J$

